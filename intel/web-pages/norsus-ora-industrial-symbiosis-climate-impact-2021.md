# Øra industriområde: Industriell symbiose og klimapåvirkning 2021

## Publication Details
- **Publication Number:** OR.17.24
- **Year:** 2024
- **Type:** Report
- **Authors:** Hanne Lerche Raadal, Pieter Callewaert, Cecilia Askham

## Overview
This report documents resource flows and climate impacts at the Øra industrial area in 2021,
comparing findings with 2018 data. The research was funded by Regional Research Fund
Oslofjordfondet and participating companies, with collaboration on energy assessments
from Fredrikstad municipality's climate initiative (Klimasats Øra).

## Energy Data (2021)
The Øra area consumed approximately **782 GWh** in 2021, distributed as:
- **Waste-based steam: 32%** (from ~130,000 tons of residual waste incineration)
- **Natural gas: 34%**
- **Electricity: 27%**
- **Oil: 4%**
- **Hot water: 4%**

## Material Flows & Industrial Symbiosis
Significant resource exchanges occurred between companies, particularly through
waste-to-energy operations:
- The area processed approximately **520,000 tons of waste**
- Roughly **390,000 tons recycled externally**
- **~24,000 tonnes iron sulfate** (jernsulfat) byproduct from Kronos Titan → Kemira's production
- **14,000 tons of recycled gypsum**
- Øra handles **43–81% of Norway's relevant waste streams**

## Climate Impact Findings
- **Direct emissions:** ~162,000 tons CO2-equivalents from energy consumption
- **Avoided emissions:** ~95,000 tons CO2-equivalents (from replacing natural gas with waste-based energy)
- **Net energy emissions:** ~67,000 tons CO2-equivalents
- **Total avoided emissions:** ~455,000 tons CO2-equivalents (from waste-based resource substitution)
- **Net climate benefit:** ~390,000 tons CO2-equivalents for 2021

For comparison, Fredrikstad municipality's total climate account was 270,786 tons
CO2-equivalents in 2021.

## Water Systems
Water systems operated more linearly than energy and waste networks:
- Glommavann consumption: **14.4 million m³**
- Potable water use: **2.5 million m³**

## Øra Area Statistics (2016 baseline)
- Approximately **145 companies**
- Approximately **2,430 employees**

## Methodological Limitations
Analysis excluded:
- Internal transport
- Waste deposition
- Incineration outside Øra
- Process emissions
- Represents only selected industrial activities
