# NORSUS Samfunnsoppdrag Sirkulærøkonomi
(NORSUS Circular Economy Mission)

## Core Mission
NORSUS has focused on circular economy since the 1990s (over 30 years), advancing
better resource utilization in cyclical systems. Only 2.4% of resource use in Norway
is truly circular (Circular Gap Report Norway, 2021).

## Three Key Principles
1. **Eliminate:** Reduce pollution and waste through durable, repairable product design
2. **Circulate:** Maintain materials in use through maintenance, repair, and reuse before recycling
3. **Regenerate:** Strengthen natural environments and promote biodiversity

## Research Methods
NORSUS employs Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) methodology as foundational to its work,
positioning itself as one of Europe's leading research environments in this field.

## Focus Areas

### Consumption Reduction & Reuse
- Food waste mapping and reduction initiatives
- Textile reuse and recycling programs
- Business model innovation for sharing economy models
- Circular indicators across sectors

### Waste Resources
- Enhanced sorting systems and collection logistics
- Food waste utilization for biogas and feed production
- Plastic and electronic waste resource recovery
- Construction material reuse

### Industrial Symbiosis
NORSUS contributed significantly to establishing the Norwegian Center for Circular
Economy (NCCE) in 2017, originating from the Øra Industrial Area in Fredrikstad.
Member businesses have prioritized waste energy utilization and closed-loop resource
management for decades.

## Key Personnel
Ole Jørgen Hanssen from NORSUS has been instrumental in this collaborative effort
and serves as Deputy Chairman of NCCE.

## Norwegian Government Priority Sectors (2021 strategy)
- Bioeconomy
- Construction
- Process industry
- Trade and service sectors
