# Kronos Titan AS - Company Research

## Company Identity
- **Name:** Kronos Titan AS
- **Org.nr:** 948 616 491
- **Address:** Titangata 1, 1630 Gamle Fredrikstad, Norway
- **Phone:** +47 69 30 90 00
- **Website:** https://www.kronosww.com/locations/fredrikstad-norway-2/
- **Founded:** 1916 (as Titan Co., by Gustav Adolf Jebsen and Peder Farup)
- **Industry:** Production of dyes and pigments (titanium dioxide)
- **Employees:** 170-180 (sources vary; 177 per proff.no, 170 per NCCE/NFEA, 180 per SNL 2020)
- **Revenue:** ~1.2 billion NOK (2020)
- **Production area:** 60,000 m2

## Ownership Structure
Kronos Titan AS is a subsidiary of:
- **Kronos Norge AS** (direct parent)
- **Kronos Denmark Aps** (intermediate holding)
- **Kronos Worldwide Inc.** (ultimate parent, Dallas, Texas, USA)

The raw material comes from sister company **Titania AS** in Sokndal (ilmenite mining).

## History
- 1916: World's first TiO2 factory built in Fredrikstad
- 1918: Production of titanium dioxide pigments began
- 1927: Acquired by National Lead Company (later NL Industries)
- 1974: KRONOS ecochem division begins marketing iron salt products
- 1989: Operations transferred to subsidiary Kronos Worldwide
- 2019: Signed partner agreement with Klimapartnere Ostfold

## Products

### Primary Product: Titanium Dioxide (TiO2)
- **Production method:** Sulfate process
- **Capacity:** 30,000 metric tonnes/year (mt/y)
- **Original capacity (1916):** 2,800 mt/y
- **Applications:** Paint, plastic, paper, rubber — used as white pigment/opacifier
- **Market position:** Scandinavia's only producer of TiO2 pigments

### Byproducts: Iron Sulfate (Jernsulfat)
- **Volume:** ~100,000 tonnes/year of various iron salt byproducts
- **Key use:** Water/wastewater treatment — serves ~5 million people in Scandinavia
- **Key customer:** Kemira Chemicals AS (neighboring company at Ora)
- **Additional use:** Thin acid residue goes to landfill for stabilizing/neutralizing hazardous inorganic waste
- **KRONOS ecochem division** markets ~700,000 tonnes of iron salts globally across all KRONOS facilities
- **Designated "samfunnsviktig funksjon" (societal-critical function):** Thin acid neutralizes fly ash from FREVAR and Kvitebjorn Bio-El incineration facilities

### Iron Sulfate Applications (via KRONOS ecochem)
- Municipal/industrial wastewater purification (precipitants/flocculants)
- Sewage sludge dewatering
- Drinking water and process water treatment
- Odor control (hydrogen sulfide elimination)
- Chromium-VI reduction in cement production
- Biogas desulfurization
- Agricultural applications
- Animal feed additives
- Iron oxide pigment production

## Energy Consumption (~310-365 GWh/year)

### Energy Breakdown (annual)
| Source | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Heavy fuel oil / LNG | ~150 GWh | Transitioning from oil to LNG |
| Steam from FREVAR/Kvitebjorn | ~130 GWh | Waste-to-energy steam |
| Electricity | ~30 GWh | Grid electricity |
| **Total** | **~310 GWh** | Some sources cite ~365 GWh (1 GWh/day) |

### Energy Profile
- Daily consumption: ~1 GWh
- Renewable percentage: 20-30% of total energy from renewable sources
- The 130 GWh steam comes from incineration of ~130,000 tons of residual waste
- Steam is high-pressure, used in production processes

### Production Process Energy Requirements
- 22 distinct production processes
- Massive rotating kilns: 55 meters long, 3-meter diameter
- Inlet temperature: 450 C
- Outlet temperature: 970 C (nearly 1,000 C)
- Gas used for drying processes and heating TiO2

## Energy Transition / Electrification

### LNG Switch (completed)
- Replaced 7,000 of 14,500 tonnes heavy fuel oil with LNG in first phase
- Emissions reduction: 5,500 tonnes CO2/year and 50 tonnes NOx/year
- Benefits: More predictable pricing than crude oil markets

### Full Electrification Plans
- Goal: Complete electric production
- Barrier: Insufficient electricity grid capacity
- Timeline: Cannot occur before 2030 at earliest
- Recent investment: ~100 million NOK in new production equipment over 5 years
- Result: 10% energy reduction while increasing production capacity

## Environmental Data

### Emissions
- CO2 reduction from LNG switch: 5,500 tonnes/year
- NOx reduction from LNG switch: 50 tonnes/year
- Historical issue: Wastewater discharge caused reduced pH in seawater, iron/titanium/chromium/vanadium overconcentrations
- 2016 incident: ~100 tonnes illegal thin acid discharge (diluted sulfuric acid with iron, chromium, titanium)

### Environmental Monitoring
- Permits and control reports: 2004-2024 (available at norskeutslipp.no)
- Water quality monitoring reports: 2015-2023
- Climate quota permits in place
- Arsenic discharge to water tracked (1.7-3.1 kg/year range, 2016-2023)

### Certifications
- **ISO 9001:2015** — Quality Management
- **ISO 14001:2015** — Environmental Management
- **ISO 50001:2018** — Energy Management

## Role in Ora Industrial Symbiosis
- Major energy consumer at Ora (~310 GWh = ~40% of Ora's total 782 GWh)
- Receives steam from FREVAR waste-to-energy plant
- Supplies iron sulfate byproduct to Kemira Chemicals
- Thin acid neutralizes fly ash from FREVAR and Kvitebjorn Bio-El
- Participant in circular economy collaboration: FREVAR -> steam -> Kronos Titan -> iron sulfate -> Kemira -> water treatment chemicals -> FREVAR wastewater plant
- Participant in SymbioLink Ora digital tour (represented by Lab Chief Oystein Ruud)
- Klimapartnere Ostfold signatory (2019)
