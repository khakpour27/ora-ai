# Kronos Titan - Kemira Chemicals: Flagship Industrial Symbiosis

## Overview
The Kronos Titan to Kemira iron sulfate flow is the flagship example of industrial symbiosis
at Ora in Fredrikstad. It represents one of Norway's longest-running and most successful
byproduct-to-feedstock exchanges, operating continuously since 1980.

## The Symbiosis Flow

### Material Flow
```
Kronos Titan AS                    Kemira Chemicals AS
(TiO2 production)                  (Water treatment chemicals)

Ilmenite ore (from Titania AS)     Iron sulfate feedstock
        |                                  |
   Sulfate process                    Chemical conversion
        |                                  |
   TiO2 pigment (30,000 t/y)      Iron chloride sulfate (coagulant)
        |                                  |
   Iron sulfate byproduct -------->  ~24,000 t/y to Kemira
   (~100,000 t/y total)                    |
        |                           Delivered to customers
   Remaining: thin acid                    |
        |                           90,000 t/y total products
   Landfill stabilization                  |
   (neutralizes hazardous waste)    Municipal wastewater plants
                                    (serves ~5 million Scandinavians)
```

## History of the Symbiosis

### Timeline
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1916 | Kronos Titan (as Titan Co.) begins TiO2 production in Fredrikstad |
| 1918 | Full-scale TiO2 pigment production begins |
| Pre-1980 | Iron sulfate byproduct discharged directly into the sea |
| 1970s | Kemira moves to the Ora area |
| 1980 | **Ferriklor** founded specifically to process Kronos Titan's iron sulfate byproduct |
| 1980s-present | Ferriklor evolves into Kemira Chemicals AS; symbiosis continues |
| 2024 | NORSUS documents ~24,000 t/y flow in 2021 baseline report |
| 2025 | Kemira expands coagulant capacity with new reactor |

### Transformation: Waste to Resource
Before the symbiosis, iron sulfate was an environmental liability — discharged into the
Glomma river and North Sea, causing water quality issues. The founding of Ferriklor in 1980
transformed this waste stream into a valuable feedstock, creating:
- A new company and jobs
- Environmental remediation (eliminated ocean dumping)
- A critical water treatment supply chain for Scandinavia

## Extended Circular Loop

The symbiosis extends beyond the two-company exchange into a broader circular system:

```
1. FREVAR incinerates ~130,000 tonnes residual waste
       |
2. Steam (~130 GWh) -> Kronos Titan production energy
       |
3. Kronos Titan produces TiO2 + iron sulfate byproduct
       |
4. Iron sulfate (~24,000 t) -> Kemira Chemicals
       |
5. Kemira produces iron chloride sulfate (coagulant)
       |
6. Coagulant -> FREVAR wastewater treatment plant + nationwide
       |
7. Treated water: nutrients -> agriculture, organics -> biogas
       |
8. Kronos thin acid -> neutralizes fly ash from FREVAR + Kvitebjorn Bio-El
```

This creates a near-closed loop where:
- Waste energy powers chemical production
- Chemical byproducts enable water treatment
- Water treatment outputs feed agriculture and energy (biogas)
- Remaining chemical waste neutralizes incineration residues

## Environmental Benefits

### Avoided Waste
- ~24,000 tonnes/year iron sulfate diverted from waste/ocean discharge
- Converted from pollutant to water purification agent
- Remaining thin acid used productively for hazardous waste stabilization

### Water Treatment Impact
- Iron chloride sulfate from Kemira cleans wastewater for ~5 million Scandinavians
- Enables nutrient recovery from sludge for agricultural use
- Enables biogas production from organic matter in wastewater

### Energy Symbiosis (Kronos Titan)
- Receives ~130 GWh/year waste-based steam from FREVAR
- This replaces fossil fuel that would otherwise be needed
- Contributes to Ora's overall avoided emissions of ~455,000 tonnes CO2-eq/year

## Economic Value

### For Kronos Titan
- Revenue from iron sulfate sales (vs. disposal cost)
- Designated societal-critical function for thin acid use
- Revenue: ~1.2 billion NOK (2020)
- 170-180 employees

### For Kemira
- Secured, local, low-cost feedstock
- Founded specifically on this symbiosis (Ferriklor, 1980)
- Revenue: ~75 million NOK
- 33 employees
- Market leader in Norwegian municipal water treatment chemicals
