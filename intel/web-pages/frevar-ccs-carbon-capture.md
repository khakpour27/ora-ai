# FREVAR KF - Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) Plans

## FREVAR's CCS Ambition
FREVAR aims to implement CCS on its waste incineration facility to:
- Help Fredrikstad reach climate targets
- Maintain important societal waste management function
- Secure energy source for industry (steam) and residents (district heating)

## Borg CO2 Cluster
- **Lead**: Borg CO2
- **Partners**: 18 companies including FREVAR KF, Borg Havn, Kvitebjorn Bio-El, Equinor, IFE, CO2 Capsol, Borregaard, Norske Skog Saugbrugs, Ostfold Energi, and others
- **Support**: CLIMIT program and host municipalities
- **Feasibility study**: "CCS-klynga paa Oera og regionalt"
- **Started**: Winter 2018/2019

## CO2 Capture Potential
- **Total potential**: ~630,000 tonnes CO2/year across three local locations
- **Significant portion**: Biogenic CO2 (bio-CCS)

## Infrastructure Requirements
- CO2 intermediate storage at Fredrikstad harbor before transport
- Connection to Oslo Carbon Cluster as interim solution
- Tie-in capability to larger terminal infrastructure
- State-coordinated receiving terminals and injection points

## Policy Advocacy
FREVAR advocates for:
- National CCS strategy including waste incineration
- Direct allocation preferred over competitive bidding
- Support for both biogenic and fossil CO2 (not just BeCCS)
- Predefined competition series with annual announcements
- Maximum contract periods for stability
- Flexibility for operational pauses
