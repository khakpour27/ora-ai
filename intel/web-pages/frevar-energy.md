# FREVAR KF - Energy Recovery Plant (Energigjenvinningsanlegg)

## Overview
The energy recovery plant converts residual waste into high-pressure steam, which serves as
energy source for both Oera industry and the Fredrikstad district heating network. The incineration
reduces waste by 80% in weight and 90% in volume.

## Waste Processing
- **Annual waste processed**: ~73,000 tonnes/year (FREVAR website)
- **Operating document capacity**: ~92,000 tonnes/year
- **Processing rate per line**: ~5.5 tonnes/hour
- **Commissioned**: 1984

Note: The NORSUS 2021 report states ~130,000 tonnes of residual waste incinerated across Oera
(this includes both FREVAR and Kvitebjorn Bio-El / SAREN Energi facilities).

## Energy & Steam Production
- **Total energy produced**: 210 GWh/year
- **Energy delivered**: ~190 GWh/year
- **Energy efficiency rate**: 99.3%
- **Annual steam delivered**: ~230,000 tonnes/year
- **Oil equivalent**: 22,000 tonnes/year

## Technical Specifications

### Boilers & Lines
- Two identical furnace shells (W+E brand)
- Two separate processing lines with individual input shafts
- Each line produces ~17.5 tonnes steam/hour

### Temperature Parameters
- Primary combustion: 900-1000 degC
- Produced steam: ~385 degC
- Delivered steam: ~320 degC
- Exhaust gas exit: ~80 degC

### Pressure Data
- Feedwater pressure: ~60 bar
- Produced steam: ~40 bar
- Delivered steam: ~18 bar

## Environmental Controls
- **Wet scrubber**: Added 1990
- **Emissions control upgrade**: 2019 (Envirotherm system)
- **Availability**: ~93.35% (continuous 6-shift operation)
- Emissions monitoring conducted twice yearly by accredited third-party testing

## Steam Delivery to Industry (from NORSUS 2021 / search results)
Key industrial steam customers on Oera:
- **Kronos Titan**: ~89 GWh steam
- **Denofa** (via Denofa Energi): receives steam from FREVAR Energigjenvinning (~62 GWh) and SAREN Energy BIO-EL (~23 GWh), forwarded to Denofa (~99 GWh total) and Adesso (~20 GWh)
- **Fredrikstad Fjernvarme**: district heating network
- **FREVAR Biogass**: ~5 GWh
- **Kemira and Reichhold**: total ~2.9 GWh

## Broader Energy Context (NORSUS 2021 report)
- Total energy consumption within Oera area: ~782 GWh (2021)
- Waste-based steam: 32% of total energy (~250 GWh)
- Natural gas: 34%
- Electricity: 27%
- Oil: 4%
- Hot water: 4%

## Industrial Symbiosis - Circular Energy Loop
- FREVAR burns residual waste, producing high-pressure steam
- Steam replaces heating oil, natural gas, and electricity for industry
- Industry returns heated cooling water to FREVAR for water treatment
- Reduced oil consumption across Oera reduces emissions of acid gases, greenhouse gases, dust, and heavy metals

## Carbon Capture (CCS) Plans
- FREVAR aims to implement CCS on waste incineration
- Part of Borg CO2 cluster (18 partner companies)
- Potential to capture ~630,000 tonnes CO2/year across three Oera locations
- Significant portion would be biogenic CO2 (bio-CCS)
- CO2 intermediate storage planned at Fredrikstad harbor
- Connection to Oslo Carbon Cluster as interim solution
