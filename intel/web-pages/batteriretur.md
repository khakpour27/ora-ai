# Batteriretur AS

## Company Overview
- **Full name:** AS Batteriretur (brand: Batteriretur®)
- **Org.nr.:** 968 840 002
- **Founded:** Late autumn 1993
- **Headquarters:** Kortbølgen 15b, 1630 Gamle Fredrikstad (Øra industrial area)
- **Phone:** +47 69 10 27 70
- **Email:** firmapost@batteriretur.no
- **Employees:** ~20
- **Revenue:** NOK 162.4 million (latest fiscal year, ~19.9% growth)
- **Tagline:** "Fremtiden er elektrisk" (The future is electric)
- **Role:** Approved collection company for all battery categories in Norway

## Corporate Structure
Batteriretur operates as two companies under the Batteriretur® brand:
- **Batteriretur AS** — main collection and recycling organization (org.nr. 968 840 002)
- **Batteriretur EV AS** — established 2014, handles EV and marine sector batteries (org.nr. 913 997 905)
- **Batteriretur Høyenergi AS** — formerly subsidiary, majority stake sold to Sortbat (Belgium) and Bebat; located at Østerøyveien 46, 3236 Sandefjord

## Battery Types Processed
- Lead-acid batteries (bly-syre)
- Alkaline/household small batteries
- Industrial batteries (vehicles, ships)
- High-energy batteries (EV batteries, lithium-ion)
- Starting batteries

## Collection Network
- Over 20,000 collection locations nationwide (retail + municipal waste facilities)
- Coverage from Lindesnes to Kinnarodden, including Svalbard
- Part of Reneos — a network of Europe's largest battery collectors

## Facilities at Øra, Fredrikstad

### Sorting Facility
- Fully automated AI sorting machine for small batteries
- Vision technology sorting ~16 batteries per second with 99.9% accuracy

### Hydrovolt (joint venture: Hydro + Northvolt)
- **Opened:** May 2022 at Øra, Fredrikstad
- **Europe's largest EV battery recycling plant**
- **Capacity:** 12,000 tonnes of battery packs/year (~25,000 EV batteries)
- **Investment:** ~120 million NOK (incl. 43.5 million NOK Enova support)
- **Operator:** Batteriretur serves as daily operator
- **Property:** Borg Havn owns the building and property
- **Target:** Recover 95% of battery content by 2025
- **Environmental permit:** Up to 15,200 tonnes lithium-ion batteries/year (Statsforvalteren)

### Hydrovolt Process
1. EV batteries dismantled
2. Copper, steel, and plastic recovered locally at Øra
3. "Black mass" generated — contains lithium, cobalt, nickel, manganese
4. Black mass shipped by boat to North Sweden (Northvolt) for reprocessing into new battery cells

### Environmental Impact
- Eliminates ~2,500 45-foot containers from boat traffic annually
- ~2 million kg CO2 reduction per year
- Northvolt target: 50% of raw materials from recycled batteries by 2030

## Recycling Processes

### Lead-acid Batteries
- Acid cleaned and reused as new battery acid, or processed for textiles/glass/detergent
- Lead refined and re-smelted → returns to battery industry for new batteries
- Lead can be recycled almost indefinitely

### Lithium-ion Batteries
- Cells mechanically separated
- Plastic, aluminium, copper extracted
- Remaining "black mass" powder contains valuable metals
- Chemical separation processes extract individual metals
- Separated metals reused in battery production

### Household Batteries
- Sorted at Fredrikstad facility
- Most shipped via container vessels to European smelting facilities
- Transformed into new batteries and/or other products

## Materials Recovered
- Lead (from lead-acid batteries)
- Cobalt, manganese, lithium, nickel (from Li-ion)
- Copper, steel, plastic, aluminium (from EV battery packs)

- Metal outputs (copper, steel, aluminium) could feed into local metal recycling ecosystem
- Plastic fractions potentially recyclable through local waste streams
- Co-located with other recyclers enables shared logistics and material exchange
- Batteriretur describes itself as fitting well among the companies at Øra industrial area

## Key Dates

- 2006: EU Battery Directive expanded
- 2014: Batteriretur EV AS established
- 2020: Hydro + Northvolt announce Hydrovolt at Batteriretur's Fredrikstad site; Statsforvalteren grants permit for high-energy battery pilot

- 2024: Høyenergi AS majority stake sold to Sortbat/Bebat
