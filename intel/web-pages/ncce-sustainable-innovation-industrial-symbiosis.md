# Bærekraftig innovasjon gjennom industriell symbiose (Sustainable Innovation through Industrial Symbiosis)

## Project Overview
Predecessor project to SymbioLink Øra. In June 2019, the Oslofjord Fund (RFF Oslofjord) awarded FREVAR KF 3,954,000 NOK for this research initiative.

## Timeline
- **Start:** 2019
- **End:** 2024
- **Funding:** 3,954,000 NOK from Oslofjordfondet (Research Fund for Oslofjord)

## Project Goal
Ensure that all material and energy resources in the Øra area are utilized as efficiently as possible through enhanced inter-business collaboration, aiming for near climate-neutral operations.

## Key Objectives
- Develop tangible innovations in partner companies
- Create collaboration models between businesses and public entities
- Build a web-based resource database for the Øra area
- Implement industrial development projects for resource optimization
- Establish networks for shared resource management

## Methodology
- Survey distributed to approximately 30 companies in the Øra Industrial Area
- Data collection on waste streams, residual products, energy consumption, water usage, and water quality specifications
- Identification of two innovation case studies for development with specific businesses
- Development of a resource database solution for efficiency tracking

## Expected Deliverables
- Dynamic resource database accessible through web platform
- Technological solutions reducing resource loss
- New business development methods promoting circular economy practices
- Documentation of industrial symbiosis models

## Real-World Symbiosis Example Documented
The project highlighted an existing circular flow:
1. FREVAR converts sorted waste into steam
2. Steam supplied to Kronos Titan and other industrial companies
3. Kronos Titan's iron sulfate by-product goes to water treatment and Kemira Chemicals
4. Kemira produces iron chloride for FREVAR
5. Treatment sludge becomes biogas and soil amendment for regional farmers

## Dissemination
Results shared on ncce.no/industriell-symbiose and presented at Ørakonferansen 2019 (September 26), which annually attracts approximately 200 participants from industry, supply sectors, and government.

## Location Context
Øra Industrial Area: approximately 2,000 hectares in Fredrikstad, employing roughly 2,900 workers.
