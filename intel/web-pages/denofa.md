# Denofa AS — Company Research

- **Org.nr.**: 987 643 935
- **Address**: Øraveien 15 B, 1630 Gamle Fredrikstad, Norway
- **Contact**: denofa@denofa.no | +47 69 39 50 00
- **Employees**: ~80 (2020, per SNL)
- **Revenue**: ~2.1 billion NOK (2020, per SNL)

- **Ownership**: AMAGGI group (Brazil), majority shareholder since 2013
- **Website**: https://www.denofa.no

## Core Business

Denofa is a Norwegian industrial company that processes non-genetically modified, sustainably certified, deforestation-free soybeans into products for the food and feed industries. The company is one of Europe's major soybean processors and represents approximately 17% of deforestation-free and sustainably certified soy imported to Nordic markets.

## Soybean Processing

- **Annual intake**: ~450,000 tonnes of soybeans per year
- **Sources**: Brazil (60–80%), Canada and USA (20–40%)
- **Main supplier**: Parent company AMAGGI (Brazil)
- **Traceability**: Identity Preservation (IP) program established 1999, tracking from individual farm seeds through supply chain
- **Certifications**: RTRS, ProTerra, FSSC 22000, GMP+/B2, Halal, Kosher, ISO 14001

## Products

### 1. Soybean Meal (Non-GMO)
- Primary product by volume
- High lysine content with easily digestible amino acids
- Heat-treated (toasted) and dried to remove anti-nutritional factors
- Salmonella and bacteria-free
- Used as ingredient in feed for cattle, pigs, and poultry

### 2. Crude Soybean Oil (Non-GMO)
- Extracted from soybeans before lecithin removal
- High unsaturated fatty acid content
- Neutral flavor with good nutritional value
- Used as concentrated energy source in animal feed, or refined to food-grade quality
- Also applicable to industrial products

### 3. Lecithin — Denothin 62 (Non-GMO)
- Phospholipid group extracted via water extraction from soybean oil
- Natural emulsifier and stabilizer
- Used in: margarine, baked goods, chocolate, coatings, dietary supplements, animal feed, cosmetics, chemical/technical industries

## Infrastructure

- **Port**: Own 510-meter deep-water quay with depth up to 10 meters
- **Port certification**: ISPS-certified (International Ship and Port Facility Security)
- **Capacity**: Can accommodate large cargo ships (bulk carriers)
- **Energy**: Own boiler house with electrode boiler and LNG gas boiler backup

## Energy Profile

### Steam Consumption
- **Total annual steam consumption**: ~100 GWh
- **External procurement**: ~80 GWh from neighboring waste incineration plants (FREVAR KF and Kvitebjørn Bio-El)
- **Own production**: 45–50 GWh from own boiler house

### Energy Sources
- **Primary**: Steam from FREVAR (waste-to-energy) and Kvitebjørn Bio-El (biomass incineration)
- **Own electrode boiler**: Primary steam generation unit, supplies both Denofa and neighboring companies
- **Gas boiler**: Backup system, operates on LNG (liquefied natural gas)

### Energy Challenges
- Insufficient reserve capacity in regional power grid prevents full use of electrode boiler
- Forced to supplement with gas boiler, creating economic challenges
- Soybean processing is classified as energy-intensive

### Energy Symbiosis
- Denofa both receives and supplies steam — it supplies steam to several neighboring companies on Øra
- Part of the Øra energy cycle: FREVAR waste → steam → Denofa + Kronos Titan + Fredrikstad Fjernvarme

## Waste Streams & Byproducts

- Soybean hulls (removed during processing)
- Wastewater streams — subject of NORSUS 2015 study on water exchange potential with FREVAR and Kronos Titan
- Cooling water — significant volumes used in processing
- Oil spill risk — incident reported on Denofa facilities (oil leakage from matoljeproduction, well contained)

## Environmental Reporting

- **ISO 14001 certified** for environmental management
- Miljødirektoratet (Norwegian Environment Agency) updating Denofa's permit under EU Industrial Emissions Directive with stricter emission limits
- Published sustainability report via Etisk handel Norge (2023 reporting year)

## Subsidiaries & Investments

- **Pronofa AS**: Spin-off company (majority owned by Denofa since 2021), pioneer in new sustainable protein sources including Ciona and Black Soldier Fly
- **Denofa Agri AS**: Vertical farming subsidiary, established May 2021
- ~1 billion NOK projected investment in alternative proteins (via Pronofa)
