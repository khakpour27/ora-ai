Combined turnover of Metallco Stene, Metallco Kabel, and Metallco Sortering: ~1 billion NOK.

## Operations

### Fragmentation Plant (Shredder)
- Receives metal-containing waste
- Crushes and separates metals from other waste materials
- Processes decommissioned vehicles, white goods, complex iron and metal
- Input and processed materials stored outdoors

### Services
- Buying and selling iron and metals
- Trade in waste (avfallshandel)
- Fragmentation of complex metal waste
- Environmental remediation of scrapped vehicles (bilvraking)
- Environmental remediation of EE-waste (electrical/electronic waste)
- Container and transport services

### Metal Types Processed
- **Ferrous metals:** Iron, steel (including structural steel, car bodies, white goods)
- **Non-ferrous metals:** Aluminium, copper, brass, stainless steel, zinc, lead
- **Complex materials:** Cables, electronics, mixed metal waste

### Processing Chain
1. Receive scrap metal, vehicles, electronics, white goods
2. Fragmentation (shredding/crushing)
3. Sorting into metal fractions (ferrous, non-ferrous, mixed)
4. Output sold to foundries, smelters, steelworks (incl. Norwegian Jøtul)
5. ~50% of Metallco group revenue from international exports

### Volumes
- Stene Stål Gjenvinning: ~50,000 tonnes recycled material annually
- Metallco Sortering (related facility): 15,000 tonnes incoming materials
- Specific Metallco Stene fragmentation capacity: not publicly disclosed

## Environmental Permit

### Current Permit
- **Issued:** 21 June 2011
- **Last modified:** 7 June 2019
- **Permitted activities:** Reception and recycling of metals, scrapped vehicles, and EE-waste

### 2019 Modification
- Removed requirement to halt operations until noise barriers installed
- Removed transportation hour restrictions (previously 07:00–19:00 Mon–Fri)
- Noise assessment confirmed emissions within permitted limits
- Authorized under Pollution Act § 18

### 2023 Permit Revision (Hearing)
- Revision required because facility falls under EU Industrial Emissions Directive (IED)
- Requires tightened emission limits for air and water aligned with BAT standards
- Hearing deadline: 16 June 2023

### Environmental Releases
- **Wastewater:** Stormwater through oil separator → discharge to Glomma river
- **Air emissions:** Point source releases from fragmentation operation

## Recent Developments

### Growth and Expansion
- Stene Stål Gjenvinning and Metallco Stene experiencing strong growth
- Contracted to receive decommissioned Oslo trams for recycling
- Sought to lease 10–15 mål (~1–1.5 hectares) from FREVAR for expansion
- FREVAR declined — needed the area for own depot (generated 30M NOK profit in 2023)
- Hiring additional staff due to increased workload

### Metallco Kabel LIBS Investment (related company, same Øra site)
- 100 million NOK investment in LIBS sorting facility
- Only LIBS facility in the Nordic region
- Processes 15 tonnes per hour
- Creates ~10 new positions
- Supplies sorted aluminium to Hydro
- Connected to AluGreen project (125M NOK, with SINTEF and Hydro)

## Industrial Symbiosis Relevance at Øra
- Central node in Øra's metal recycling ecosystem
- Vertically integrated with Stene Stål Gjenvinning (collection/sorting) and Metallco Kabel (cable processing)
- Metal fractions output to foundries, smelters, steelworks
- Adjacent to Batteriretur/Hydrovolt — potential synergy with metal fractions from battery recycling
- Co-located with FREVAR (waste-to-energy), Kronos Titan, Kemira, etc.
- Shares logistics infrastructure via Borg Havn
- Exemplifies industrial symbiosis: material flow optimization across interconnected operations
- SymbioLink Øra 2025 participant

## Energy Data
