## Operations
Metallco Kabel is an independent unit within the Metallco recycling group, specializing in cable recycling (gjenvinning av kabel). The company sorts and material-recycles used cables, separating copper and aluminum from plastic insulation/binding material.

### Process
1. Cables received from Metallco locations across Norway
2. Cables are ground up (granulated)
3. Insulation and metal (copper/aluminum) are mechanically separated
4. Recovered copper is sold as raw material to chemical industry and smelting works
5. Aluminum fractions sorted and sold
6. Cable plastic (6,000-8,000 tonnes/year) is a byproduct being explored for new uses

### Materials Processed
- **Copper cables** (free of grease, reinforcement such as iron/lead/aluminum)
- **Aluminum cables** (free of grease, reinforcement such as iron/lead)
- **Output:** Pure copper, aluminum fractions, cable plastic

## Major Investment: LIBS Sorting Facility (2022)
- **Investment:** 100 million NOK
- **Technology:** LIBS (Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy) sorting facility
- **Distinction:** Only LIBS facility in the Nordic region
- **Capacity:** 15 tonnes per hour
- **Purpose:** Process refined material fractions domestically instead of exporting
- **Benefit:** Delivers purer fractions (renere fraksjoner) to customers
- **New jobs:** 10 additional employees
- **Timeline:** Equipment delivery and startup expected before December 2022
- **Context:** Part of the AluGreen project (125 million NOK initiative for circular aluminum products using recycled material and renewable energy)

## Environmental Impact
- Recycling 1 tonne of aluminum saves 6 tonnes of bauxite ore
- CO2 reduction: 0.5 tonnes vs 9.5 tonnes for virgin aluminum (saving 9 tonnes CO2 per tonne recycled)
- All metals can be melted repeatedly without quality deterioration
