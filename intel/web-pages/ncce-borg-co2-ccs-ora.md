# Borg CO2: CCS Cluster on Øra and Regionally

## Project Overview
Collaborative initiative to establish carbon capture and storage (CCS) infrastructure in the Fredrikstad, Sarpsborg, and Halden region.

## Participants & Partners (18 partners)
Core partners:
- Norske Skog Saugbrugs
- FREVAR
- Sarpsborg Avfallsenergi
- Kvitebjørn Bio-El
- Borregaard
- Borg Havn (hosting the CO2 terminal)

Additional partners: Equinor, Fortum Oslo Varme, IFE, CO2 Capsol, Acinor, Compact Carbon Capture, and others from process industry, waste management, logistics, and academia.

## CO2 Capture Capacity
Target: up to 630,000 tons of CO2 annually from five regional industrial sources.

## Timeline
- Project initiation: Late 2017/early 2018
- Target operational date: First half of 2024
- Revenue expectations: Returns projected beginning 2025

## Infrastructure
- CO2 transported from regional industries to shared terminal at Borg Havn (Øra, Fredrikstad)
- Then via specialized ships to receiving terminal in Øygarden
- Storage 2,600 meters below the seabed in the North Sea
- First collaboration agreement between Northern Lights and an industrial cluster

## Economics
- Cost: 1,250 NOK per ton captured CO2
- Expected to create 30-50 direct jobs
- Plus approximately 200 additional positions in maintenance and related services
