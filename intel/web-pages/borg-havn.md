# Borg Havn IKS — Company Research

## Company Overview

- **Full name**: Borg Havn IKS (Interkommunalt Selskap / Inter-municipal Company)
- **Org.nr.**: 970 937 099
- **Address**: Gamle Fredrikstad (Øra area)
- **Contact**: firmapost@borg-havn.no | +47 69 35 89 00
- **Type**: Multi-use port authority and logistics operator
- **Ownership**: Inter-municipal company owned by Fredrikstad, Sarpsborg, and Hvaler municipalities
- **Website**: https://www.borg-havn.no
- **Financial result (2024)**: Record surplus of 52.2 million NOK

## Core Business

Borg Havn is a versatile multi-user port that has developed over 25+ years from a traditional port authority into a professional logistics and business operator. It is designated as a trunk port (stamnetthavn) in Norway's national trunk network for sea, land, and air transport, serving as a strategic hub between the national maritime system and international destinations.

## Geographic Coverage

Borg Havn manages quays and waterfront areas across three municipalities:
- **Fredrikstad** — Øraterminalen (main cargo terminal)
- **Sarpsborg** — Alvim terminal
- **Hvaler** — Utgårdskilen

Coverage extends from Utgårdskilen in the south to Sarpsborg in the north.

## Port Infrastructure

### Øraterminalen (Main Terminal at Øra)
- **Classification**: Multi-purpose terminal, the heart of Borg Havn's operations
- **Total quay length**: 1,125 meters
- **Water depth**: 9.8–11.3 meters
- **Vessel capacity**: Can accommodate vessels up to 250 meters in length
- **Ro-Ro ramps**: 2 available for roll-on/roll-off operations
- **Utilities**: Water and electricity access at quay facilities

### Total Port Infrastructure
- **Total quayage**: Over 2,000 meters across all locations
- **ISPS certification**: Security certified

## Crane & Equipment

### Liebherr LPS 420 E (Installed 2022–2023)
- **Type**: Electric harbor crane (first LPS 420 E in the Nordic region)
- **Manufacturer**: Liebherr
- **Contract value**: EUR 5.3 million (signed March 2022)
- **Total weight**: 525 tonnes
- **Maximum lifting capacity**: 124 tonnes
- **Capacity at full extension**: 34.5 tonnes
- **Boom length**: 48 meters
- **Operation**: Wire-controlled (no hydraulic cylinders in boom)
- **Power**: 100% electric, connected to Borg Havn's high-voltage grid (potentially solar-powered)
- **Rail-mounted**: Runs on rails like a ship-to-shore crane
- **Handles**: Breakbulk cargo, dry bulk, containers, project cargo
- **Environmental**: Low noise, zero direct emissions

## Recent Developments

- **Fredrikstad Cargo Terminal (FCT)**: New terminal established in Borg Havn, part of Scan Logistic Partner AS and Husoy Terminal AS network
- **New maritime route (2024)**: Door-to-door transport via Samskip and Viasea connecting to Tilbury and Rotterdam
- **Electric crane (2022–2023)**: First Liebherr LPS 420 E in Nordic region, part of zero-emission terminal goal
- **Borg Havn Eiendom AS**: Subsidiary for property management (org.nr. 920 327 249)

## Environmental & Sustainability

- Goal: Become a zero-emissions terminal
- Electric crane replacing diesel-powered alternatives
- Solar panels on port facilities
- Focus on minimizing noise, dust, and emissions
- Renewable energy solutions under development

## Connection to Øra Industrial Ecosystem

Borg Havn is a central logistics hub for the Øra industrial area:

| Connection | Partner | Description |
