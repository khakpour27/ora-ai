# Stort biogasspotensial i Nedre Glomma-regionen
(Large Biogas Potential in the Nedre Glomma Region)

## Key Findings
Increased biogas production at the Øra industrial area could contribute to a climate
benefit of up to **8,400 tons of CO2 equivalents per year** based on current facility operations.

## Expansion Scenarios
1. **Reference scenario:** Current FREVAR biogas facility operations
2. **2027 scenario:** New facility operational
3. **2030 scenario:** Third biogas facility established, yielding a climate benefit of
   up to **11,400 tons of CO2-equivalents** (8,400-ton annual increase beyond current levels)

## Energy Capacity
The region demonstrates potential to expand biogas production equivalent to
**100 GWh capacity**, with applications across transport and industrial sectors
as replacement for natural gas and diesel fuels.

## Key Stakeholders
- Biogass Oslofjord
- Østfold County Council
- Fredrikstad Municipality
- FREVAR

## Agricultural Resources
The report emphasizes securing livestock manure and agricultural residues as critical
feedstock, aligning with Norway's Climate Agreement target requiring **30% of livestock
manure processing through biogas plants by 2030**.

## Context
This represents ~4% of Fredrikstad municipality's total emissions, making biogas
a significant contributor to local climate goals.
