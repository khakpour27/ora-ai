# FREVAR: Motoren i energikretsløpet på Øra (The Motor in the Energy Cycle at Øra)

## Energy Recovery Operations
FREVAR operates an energy recovery facility established in 1984 that converts residual waste into high-pressure steam. This facility forms the core of Øra's industrial symbiosis network.

## Energy Production Process
The installation transforms waste into high-pressure steam which serves as an energy source for both industry and Fredrikstad's district heating network. This steam replaces traditional energy sources like heating oil, natural gas, and electricity for local industrial users.

## Energy Distribution and Partnerships
- Started collaboration with Kronos Titan and Denofa as large energy customers
- Initiated partnership with Fredrikstad Fjernvarme (district heating)
- Receives heated cooling water from industry, which it reuses in water treatment processes (circular resource flow)

## Biogas Production
- Biogas facility constructed in 2014
- Processes food waste and organic materials
- Produces upgraded biogas for fuel use
- Distribution through Gasum partnerships, reaching both other parts of Norway and Sweden
- Production doubled from initial capacity through infrastructure expansion

## FREVAR Operations Overview
- Wastewater treatment
- Water production (clean drinking water)
- Energy recovery from waste
- Waste management and sorting
- Biogas production
- Approximately 90 staff members across all operations
