# Metallco investerer 100 millioner i sirkulærøkonomien på Øra

## Investment Overview
Metallco Kabel is investing 100 million Norwegian kroner in a new LIBS (Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy) sorting facility at Øra - the only one of its kind in the Nordic region.

## Current Operations
- Established at Øra in 2018
- Processes approximately 14,000 tonnes of used cables annually
- Primary focus: extracting aluminium and copper from waste cable materials for reintegration into manufacturing cycles

## Environmental Impact
- Recycling one tonne of aluminium conserves six tonnes of bauxite ore
- CO2 emissions reduced by 9 tonnes per tonne of recycled aluminium compared to virgin aluminium
- Recovered aluminium supplies Hydro for Norwegian production

## LIBS Technology
- Enables domestic material refinement rather than exporting raw fractions internationally
- Processes 15 tonnes per hour
- Creates approximately 10 new positions, with expansion planned

## Strategic Partnerships
Connected to the "AluGreen" project:
- 125-million-kroner initiative
- Developing circular aluminium products from recycled materials and renewable energy
- Running through 2024
- Partners include SINTEF and Hydro

## Market Context
Aluminium demand projections indicate over 50% growth by 2050.
