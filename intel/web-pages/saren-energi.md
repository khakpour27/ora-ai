# SAREN Energy AS

## Company Overview

SAREN Energy AS is a Norwegian district heating and waste-to-energy platform. The company manages waste that cannot or should not be recycled and utilizes the surplus energy to provide heat, steam, and electricity.

- **Organization Number:** 925 200 719
- **Headquarters:** Haakon VIIs gate 2, 0161 Oslo, Norway
- **Ownership:** PATRIZIA Infrastructure (European infrastructure investor)
- **Established:** 2020 (via merger of Sarpsborg Avfallsenergi AS and Kvitebjorn Bio-El AS)
- **Total platform value:** NOK 3.5 billion (EUR 350 million)
- **Total energy delivery:** Up to 525 GWh/year across all plants
- **Management team:** 9 listed employees at group level

A third waste line was commissioned in December 2024, nearly doubling the plant capacity.

### Energy Output Streams

1. **District Heating (Fjernvarme):** Hot water to the Fredrikstad Fjernvarme network for residential and commercial heating
2. **Industrial Steam:** Delivered via pipeline (operated by FREVAR KF) to local industries at Ora
3. **Electricity:** Generated through onsite turbine/generator and supplied to the public grid

### Operations Model

The plant is co-located with FREVAR KF at Habornveien 61 on Ora. FREVAR KF operates and maintains the SAREN Energy BIO-EL plant through long-term agreements. This means the actual workforce running the plant is FREVAR staff, which explains the low registered employee count (1-4) at Bio-EL AS itself.

### Waste Sourcing

More than 2/3 of the waste feedstock comes from the UK, transported via efficient logistics through Borg Havn (Borg Harbor). The UK faces a shortage of modern waste treatment capacity, making export to Norwegian facilities economically viable.

The plant uses approximately 1,600 tonnes of virgin sand annually from Baskarp, Sweden for its processes. A research project is testing ironworks slag as a replacement, potentially reducing virgin sand consumption to 600 tonnes/year.

## Connections to Other Ora Companies

### Steam Customers (via FREVAR pipeline network)

SAREN Energy BIO-EL and FREVAR together supply industrial steam to:

- **Denofa AS** - Uses approximately 100 GWh of steam annually for soybean processing
- **Kronos Titan AS** - Steam for titanium dioxide production
- **Kemira Chemicals AS** - Steam for water treatment chemical production
- **Reichold AS** - Steam for chemical production (resin manufacturing)

### Energy Network Partners

- **FREVAR KF** - Operates the BIO-EL plant; co-located facility; manages steam pipeline distribution
- **Fredrikstad Fjernvarme AS** - Receives hot water for district heating distribution to ~3,000 households
- **Borg Havn IKS** - Port logistics for waste import (primarily from UK)

### SymbioLink Ora (2025)

SAREN Energi is a participant in the SymbioLink Ora project led by NCCE and NORSUS, which runs from January-December 2025. The project aims to optimize resource utilization through expanded industrial symbiosis in the Ora industrial area.

## Key Significance for Ora Ecosystem

SAREN Energy BIO-EL is a cornerstone of the Ora energy network:
- Converts waste into three energy streams (heat, steam, electricity)
- Provides critical industrial steam to four major Ora companies
- Supplies baseload heat to the Fredrikstad district heating network
- Imports waste via Borg Havn, connecting port operations to energy production
- December 2024 capacity expansion nearly doubles energy contribution to the ecosystem
