## Project Overview

**SymbioLink Øra (2025)** is the current initiative optimizing industrial symbiosis at the Øra industrial area in Fredrikstad, Norway — often called "sirkulærhovedstaden" (Norway's circular economy capital).

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Lead organizations** | NCCE (Norwegian Center for Circular Economy) + NORSUS |
| **Duration** | January–December 2025 |
| **Funding** | Østfold fylkeskommune |
| **Project lead** | Benedikte Bekkevold Hansen |
| **Predecessor** | "Sustainable Innovation through Industrial Symbiosis" (2019–2024, 3.9 MNOK from Oslofjordfondet) |
| **Governance** | Coordinator within Fredrikstad Business Council + steering committee |
| **Participants** | 9 organizations: FREVAR, Borg Havn, Metallco Stene, SAREN Energi, Metallco Kabel, Stene Stål, Fredrikstad Fjernvarme, NG Metall, Visit Fredrikstad & Hvaler |

## Energy Flows

### Aggregate (2021)
- **Total consumption:** ~782 GWh/year
- **Mix:** Natural gas 34% (~266 GWh), Waste-based steam 32% (~250 GWh), Electricity 27% (~211 GWh), Oil 4% (~31 GWh), Hot water 4% (~31 GWh)
- **Waste incinerated:** ~130,000 tonnes/year (FREVAR + SAREN combined)

### FREVAR Energy Recovery Plant
- Operational since 1984; ~73,000 t waste/yr; ~210 GWh produced, ~190 GWh delivered
- Steam: ~230,000 t/yr at ~320°C, ~18 bar
- Energy efficiency: 99.3%

### SAREN BIO-EL Plant
- Expanded December 2024: 22→37 MW, 60,000→105,000 t/yr waste capacity
- Gross energy: ~170 GWh (original) → ~300 GWh (expanded)
- >2/3 of waste imported from UK via Borg Havn

### Biogas (FREVAR)
- Raw biogas: ~4 million m³/yr; upgraded biomethane: ~2.5 million m³/yr
- Distributed via Gasum to Norway and Sweden
- Regional potential: 100 GWh capacity (Nedre Glomma)
- CO₂ benefit: 8,400 t/yr current, up to 11,400 t/yr by 2030

## Symbiosis Opportunities

### Existing (documented, operational)
1. **FREVAR steam network** — waste→steam→industry (since 1984)
2. **SAREN BIO-EL steam** — waste→steam→Denofa/Fjernvarme (since 2008)
3. **Kronos→Kemira iron sulfate** — byproduct→feedstock (since 1980)
4. **Fredrikstad Fjernvarme** — waste heat→3,000+ households
5. **FREVAR Biogas** — food waste→biomethane→fuel network
6. **Kronos thin acid→fly ash neutralization** — cross-company waste treatment
7. **Cross-company metal recycling** — Stene Stål→foundries/smelters

### Planned/Potential
1. **Borg CO₂ CCS Cluster** — 630,000 t CO₂/yr from 18 partners, CO₂ terminal at Borg Havn
2. **Expanded biogas** — 100 GWh regional potential
3. **Kronos Titan electrification** — replace ~150 GWh oil/LNG with electricity (post-2030)
4. **Cable plastic utilization** — 6,000-8,000 t/yr from Metallco Kabel seeking new uses
5. **Water symbiosis** — FREVAR-Denofa-Kronos water exchange (identified in NORSUS 2015 study)
6. **Ironworks slag replacing sand** — SAREN BIO-EL testing to cut virgin sand from 1,600→600 t/yr
7. **Alternative proteins** — Pronofa (Denofa subsidiary) ~1B NOK investment

## Data Gaps

### Critical (blocks app visualization)
- **Batteriretur AS**: No volume data, employee count uncertain, energy unknown
- **Metallco Stene AS**: No separate throughput or financial data (merged with Stene Stål group)
- **Individual company CO₂ emissions**: Only area-wide aggregates available
- **Kemira energy consumption**: Not published
- **FREVAR detailed steam flow to Fredrikstad Fjernvarme**: Only combined with SAREN

### Important (enriches analysis)
- **EWC waste classification codes**: Not found for any waste streams
- **Seasonal energy variation**: No quarterly/seasonal breakdowns
- **Transport methods/distances**: Between companies not documented
- **Economic values of symbiosis flows**: Pricing of steam, iron sulfate etc. not public
- **Water quality parameters**: Referenced in NORSUS 2015 study but not extracted
- **Historical trends**: Only 2021 baseline available; no multi-year comparison

### Nice-to-have
- **Additional companies**: Jotun, Polynt, Reichhold, Adesso, Sirkel AS — minimal data
- **Employment breakdown**: Many companies report group totals not site-specific
- **Financial details**: Many companies privately held with limited public data
