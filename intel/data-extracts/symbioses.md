# Symbiosis Connections - Ora Industrial Area

Data extracted from intel/web-pages/ on 2026-03-16.
Primary data source: NORSUS Report OR.17.24 (2021 baseline) and NCCE project documentation.

## Existing Symbioses

### 1. FREVAR Waste-to-Energy Steam Network
- **Type**: energy
- **Companies**: FREVAR KF (supplier) -> Kronos Titan, Denofa, Fredrikstad Fjernvarme, Kemira, Reichhold, FREVAR Biogass
- **Description**: FREVAR incinerates ~73,000 t/yr residual waste and produces high-pressure steam (~210 GWh/yr produced, ~190 GWh/yr delivered). Steam replaces fossil fuels (heating oil, natural gas) for industrial processes.
- **Volume**: ~190 GWh/yr delivered; ~230,000 tonnes steam/yr
- **Key delivery breakdown**:
  - Kronos Titan: ~89 GWh
  - Denofa Energi: ~62 GWh
  - FREVAR Biogass: ~5 GWh
  - Kemira + Reichhold: ~2.9 GWh
  - Fredrikstad Fjernvarme: portion of ~88+ GWh total
- **CO2 reduction**: ~95,000 t CO2-eq/yr (avoided by replacing natural gas with waste-based steam)
- **Operational since**: 1984
- **Source**: frevar-energy.md, norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md, ncce-frevar-energy-motor-ora.md

### 2. SAREN Energy BIO-EL Steam Supply
- **Type**: energy
- **Companies**: SAREN Energy BIO-EL / Kvitebjorn Bio-El (supplier) -> Denofa Energi, Fredrikstad Fjernvarme, industrial customers
- **Description**: Waste-to-energy plant co-located with FREVAR. Incinerates residual waste (>2/3 from UK) and delivers steam to Ora industries and district heating. Operated by FREVAR staff.
- **Volume**: Original: 170 GWh/yr gross. Expanded (Dec 2024+): ~300 GWh/yr gross. Delivers ~23 GWh to Denofa Energi.
- **Capacity**: Original 60,000 t/yr waste, expanded to 105,000 t/yr
- **CO2 reduction**: Included in area-wide figures
- **Operational since**: 2008
- **Source**: saren-energi.md, norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md

### 3. Denofa Energi Steam Hub
- **Type**: energy
- **Companies**: Denofa Energi (hub) receiving from FREVAR + SAREN, distributing to Denofa AS and Adesso
- **Description**: Denofa Energi acts as an intermediary, receiving steam from waste-to-energy plants and supplementing with own electrode/gas boilers, then forwarding to Denofa and neighbors.
- **Volume**: ~99 GWh to Denofa, ~20 GWh to Adesso. Denofa total consumption ~100 GWh. Own production: 45-50 GWh.
- **CO2 reduction**: Part of area-wide steam replacement figures
- **Source**: denofa.md, frevar-denofa-steam.md, norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md

### 4. Kronos Titan -> Kemira Iron Sulfate Exchange
- **Type**: material (byproduct-to-feedstock)
- **Companies**: Kronos Titan AS -> Kemira Chemicals AS
- **Description**: Kronos Titan produces TiO2 via sulfate process, generating iron sulfate as byproduct. ~24,000 t/yr goes to adjacent Kemira, which converts it into iron chloride sulfate (coagulant) for municipal water treatment. Founded specifically on this flow (Ferriklor, 1980).
- **Volume**: ~24,000 t/yr iron sulfate (of ~100,000 t/yr total byproduct)
- **CO2 reduction**: Included in area-wide avoided emissions. Avoids 24,000 t/yr waste to landfill/ocean.
- **Operational since**: 1980 (46 years continuous operation)
- **Distance**: ~100-200m between facilities
- **Source**: kronos-kemira-symbiosis.md, kemira-chemicals.md, norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md

### 5. Kronos Titan Thin Acid -> Fly Ash Neutralization
- **Type**: material (waste-to-utility)
- **Companies**: Kronos Titan AS -> FREVAR KF, Kvitebjorn Bio-El
- **Description**: Kronos Titan's thin acid residue (diluted sulfuric acid with iron, chromium, titanium) is used to neutralize and stabilize fly ash from waste incineration at FREVAR and Kvitebjorn Bio-El. Designated as a societal-critical function (samfunnsviktig funksjon).
- **Volume**: Unknown
- **CO2 reduction**: Unknown (avoids alternative hazardous waste treatment)
- **Source**: kronos-titan.md, kronos-kemira-symbiosis.md

### 6. Kemira -> FREVAR Wastewater Treatment Chemicals
- **Type**: material (product-to-service)
- **Companies**: Kemira Chemicals AS -> FREVAR KF wastewater plant (+ nationwide municipal plants)
- **Description**: Kemira supplies iron chloride sulfate coagulant to FREVAR's wastewater treatment plant and nationwide. Completes the circular loop: FREVAR steam -> Kronos Titan -> iron sulfate -> Kemira -> coagulant -> FREVAR wastewater. Treatment sludge nutrients go to agriculture, organics go to biogas.
- **Volume**: 90,000 t/yr total products nationwide; FREVAR-specific volume unknown
- **CO2 reduction**: Enables water treatment for ~5 million Scandinavians
- **Source**: kemira-chemicals.md, kronos-kemira-symbiosis.md

### 7. Fredrikstad Fjernvarme District Heating
- **Type**: energy (heat distribution)
- **Companies**: FREVAR + SAREN Energy -> Fredrikstad Fjernvarme AS -> ~3,000+ households and buildings
- **Description**: 95% of district heating energy comes from waste heat recovery at Ora incineration plants. Distributes hot water via underground pipe network for space heating, floor heating, ventilation, and domestic hot water.
- **Volume**: >88 GWh heat + 2 GWh cooling annually
- **Proposed expansion**: 160 GWh production, 100 MW power
- **CO2 reduction**: Replaces individual fossil fuel heating in buildings
- **Operational since**: 2000
- **Source**: fredrikstad-fjernvarme.md, norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md

### 8. FREVAR Circular Water Reuse
- **Type**: material (water)
- **Companies**: Industrial customers -> FREVAR KF
- **Description**: FREVAR receives heated cooling water from industry and reuses it in water treatment processes, creating a circular water flow.
- **Volume**: Unknown (area total: 14.4 million m3/yr raw water, 2.5 million m3/yr potable water)
- **Source**: ncce-frevar-energy-motor-ora.md, frevar-energy.md

### 9. FREVAR Biogas -> Transport Fuel
- **Type**: energy + material
- **Companies**: FREVAR KF (producer) -> Gasum (distributor) -> Transport sector (Norway + Sweden)
- **Description**: FREVAR biogas plant processes 30,000 t/yr food waste and organic substrate into biogas. Upgraded biomethane (2.5 million m3/yr) used as fuel for buses, waste collection vehicles, and gas-powered vehicles.
- **Volume**: ~4 million m3/yr raw biogas, ~2.5 million m3/yr upgraded biomethane
- **CO2 reduction**: 8,400 t CO2-eq/yr (current); potential 11,400 t CO2-eq/yr (2030 scenario)
- **Operational since**: 2014
- **Source**: frevar-biogas.md, norsus-biogas-potential-nedre-glomma.md

### 10. FREVAR Biogas Digestate -> Agriculture
- **Type**: material
- **Companies**: FREVAR KF -> Regional farmers
- **Description**: Anaerobic digestion process produces nutrient-rich digestate (bioresources) used as soil amendment in agriculture.
- **Volume**: Unknown
- **CO2 reduction**: Replaces synthetic fertilizer
- **Source**: frevar-biogas.md, kronos-kemira-symbiosis.md

### 11. Gypsum Recycling
- **Type**: material
- **Companies**: Gipsgjenvinning -> Gyproc
- **Description**: Recycled gypsum from waste streams processed and supplied to Gyproc for new gypsum board production.
- **Volume**: ~14,000 t/yr
- **CO2 reduction**: Unknown (avoids virgin gypsum extraction)
- **Source**: norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md

### 12. Stene Stal -> Foundries/Smelters Metal Supply Chain
- **Type**: material
- **Companies**: Stene Stal Gjenvinning -> Jotul ASA, Kronos Titan, Ulefos Jernvaerk, Hafsil AS, Celsa Armeringsstal
- **Description**: Stene Stal processes ~50,000 t/yr scrap metal (vehicles, industrial waste, steel constructions) and delivers sorted fractions to foundries, smelters, and steel works. All Norwegian Jotul wood stoves use exclusively Stene Stal iron scrap.
- **Volume**: ~50,000 t/yr total throughput
- **CO2 reduction**: 1.0-1.3 t CO2 saved per tonne iron recycled; ~10 t CO2 per tonne aluminum; ~20 t CO2 per tonne copper
- **Source**: stene-stal.md

### 13. Metallco Kabel Cable Recycling -> Metal Supply Chain
- **Type**: material
- **Companies**: Metallco network (nationwide) -> Metallco Kabel -> Chemical industry, smelters (copper), Hydro (aluminum)
- **Description**: Processes ~14,000 t/yr used cables by granulating and mechanically separating copper and aluminum from plastic insulation. LIBS technology (100 MNOK investment) enables domestic processing of refined fractions.
- **Volume**: ~14,000 t/yr input (target 18,000+); 6,000-8,000 t/yr cable plastic byproduct
- **CO2 reduction**: 9 t CO2 saved per tonne recycled aluminum vs. virgin
- **Source**: metallco-kabel.md, ncce-metallco-investment-ora.md

### 14. Metallco Stene/Kabel/Stene Stal Ownership Cluster
- **Type**: infrastructure + material
- **Companies**: Stene Stal Gjenvinning (parent), Metallco Stene, Metallco Kabel, Hafsil AS, NorSer Recycling
- **Description**: Co-located group of companies under shared ownership enabling coordinated metal recycling, cable processing, smelting, and equipment maintenance. Shared logistics and material flows.
- **Volume**: Combined ~1 billion NOK revenue
- **Source**: stene-stal.md, metallco-kabel.md

### 15. NG Metall Metal Recycling Hub
- **Type**: material
- **Companies**: NG Metall/Nordic (Fredrikstad) -> International smelters and steel works
- **Description**: Norway's largest metal recycling facility with 140 t/hr shredder. Processes scrapped vehicles, construction materials, industrial waste, electronic waste, batteries, cables. Co-located with other Ora recyclers near Borg Havn for export logistics.
- **Volume**: ~250,000 t/yr (Norwegian operations); 1,000 cars/day equivalent
- **CO2 reduction**: 984,374 t CO2 avoided (2024, all 9 NG Nordic facilities)
- **Source**: ng-metall.md

### 16. Borg Havn Logistics Hub
- **Type**: infrastructure + service
- **Companies**: Borg Havn IKS serves Denofa, Kronos Titan, FREVAR, Metallco, Batteriretur, and all Ora companies
- **Description**: Multi-use port handling ~2.78 million t/yr cargo. Provides import/export logistics for raw materials, finished products, waste streams, and recycled materials. Electric crane (124t capacity). ISPS certified.
- **Volume**: ~2.78 million t/yr total cargo; ~34,400 TEU containers
- **Key flows**: Denofa soybeans (450,000 t/yr import), UK waste to SAREN (import), recycled metals (export), Kronos TiO2 (export/import)
- **Source**: borg-havn.md

## Planned/Potential Symbioses

### P1. Borg CO2 - Carbon Capture and Storage Cluster
- **Type**: infrastructure + material (CO2)
- **Companies**: 18 partners including FREVAR, Borg Havn, Kvitebjorn Bio-El, Equinor, Borregaard, Norske Skog Saugbrugs, and others
- **Description**: Planned CCS cluster capturing CO2 from industrial sources at Ora and regionally. CO2 transported to shared terminal at Borg Havn, then by ship to Oygarden for storage 2,600m below North Sea seabed. First collaboration agreement between Northern Lights and an industrial cluster.
- **Volume**: Up to 630,000 t CO2/yr across three local locations (significant biogenic CO2)
- **Cost**: 1,250 NOK per tonne captured CO2
- **Jobs**: 30-50 direct + ~200 maintenance/related
- **Status**: Under development since 2017/2018. Feasibility study complete. Target operations delayed.
- **Source**: ncce-borg-co2-ccs-ora.md, ncce-ccs-cluster-ora-regional.md, frevar-ccs-carbon-capture.md, norsus-ccs-cluster-ora.md

### P2. Ironworks Slag Replacing Virgin Sand at SAREN BIO-EL
- **Type**: material (waste-to-feedstock)
- **Companies**: Unknown ironworks -> SAREN Energy BIO-EL
- **Description**: Research project testing ironworks slag as replacement for virgin sand in incineration processes. Could reduce virgin sand consumption from 1,600 t/yr to 600 t/yr.
- **Status**: Research/testing
- **Source**: saren-energi.md

### P3. Metallco Kabel Cable Plastic Valorization
- **Type**: material (byproduct-to-product)
- **Companies**: Metallco Kabel -> Unknown (new value chain)

### P4. Water Exchange Between FREVAR, Denofa, Kronos Titan
- **Type**: material (water)
- **Companies**: FREVAR KF, Denofa AS, Kronos Titan AS
- **Description**: NORSUS 2015 study identified potential for exchanging water streams among the three companies. Water systems currently operate more linearly than energy/waste networks. Cooling water demand is substantial. Exchange could reduce total wastewater discharge.
- **Status**: Studied (2015), not fully implemented
- **Source**: norsus-water-flows-frevar-denofa-kronos.md

### P5. Expanded Biogas Production (Nedre Glomma Region)
- **Type**: energy
- **Companies**: FREVAR, regional biogas operators, agricultural sector
- **Description**: Potential to expand biogas production to 100 GWh capacity using livestock manure and agricultural residues. Would add 8,400 t CO2-eq/yr climate benefit beyond current operations (2030 scenario: 11,400 t CO2-eq/yr).
- **Status**: Study completed, expansion scenarios defined
- **Source**: norsus-biogas-potential-nedre-glomma.md

### P6. Kronos Titan Full Electrification
- **Type**: energy
- **Companies**: Kronos Titan AS, grid operator
- **Description**: Complete replacement of fossil fuels (LNG) with electric production. Would eliminate ~5,500 t CO2/yr remaining from LNG use. Barrier: insufficient electricity grid capacity. Cannot occur before 2030.

- **Type**: material (CO2 as feedstock)
- **Companies**: CCS cluster participants
- **Description**: Beyond storage, captured CO2 could become feedstock for other industrial processes (CCU), adding a new symbiosis dimension to existing material and energy networks.

- **Description**: Using waste sludge from Norwegian aquaculture as feed for insect larvae (Black Soldier Fly) production. Tunicates filter nutrient pollution from water while producing omega-3 rich protein. ~1 billion NOK investment.

## The Triangular Core Symbiosis

The most iconic symbiosis loop at Ora involves three companies in a near-closed cycle:

```
    FREVAR KF
   /         \
  steam       wastewater
  (~89 GWh)   treatment
  |            chemicals
  v            ^
Kronos       Kemira
Titan   -->  Chemicals
  iron sulfate
  (~24,000 t/yr)
```

Extended circular loop:
1. FREVAR incinerates ~130,000 t waste -> produces steam
2. Steam -> Kronos Titan (for TiO2 production energy)
3. Kronos Titan produces TiO2 + iron sulfate byproduct
4. Iron sulfate (~24,000 t) -> Kemira Chemicals
5. Kemira produces iron chloride sulfate (coagulant)
6. Coagulant -> FREVAR wastewater treatment + nationwide
7. Treated water: nutrients -> agriculture, organics -> biogas
8. Kronos thin acid -> neutralizes fly ash from FREVAR + Kvitebjorn Bio-El
