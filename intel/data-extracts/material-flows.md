## Waste Streams by Company

### FREVAR KF
- **Residual waste incinerated (FREVAR alone)**: ~73,000 t/yr (capacity 92,000 t/yr)
- **Total residual waste incinerated across Ora (FREVAR + SAREN/Kvitebjorn)**: ~130,000 t/yr
- Current handling: Energy recovery (waste-to-energy)
- Output: High-pressure steam, bottom ash, fly ash
- Fly ash neutralized by Kronos Titan thin acid

### FREVAR Biogas
- **Feedstock capacity**: 30,000 t/yr total substrate
  - Packaged substrate: 6,000 t/yr
  - Liquid feedstock: 24,000 t/yr
  - Sludge: up to 2,000 t/yr
- **Raw biogas output**: ~4 million m3/yr
- **Upgraded biomethane**: ~2.5 million m3/yr
- **Digestate**: Nutrient-rich soil amendment sent to agriculture
- Distribution: Via Gasum to Norway and Sweden

### SAREN Energy BIO-EL (Kvitebjorn Bio-El)
- **Waste incinerated (original capacity)**: 60,000 t/yr
- **Waste incinerated (expanded, Dec 2024+)**: 105,000 t/yr
- Waste source: >2/3 from UK
- Uses ~1,600 t/yr virgin sand (research ongoing to replace with ironworks slag, reducing to 600 t/yr)

### Kronos Titan AS
- **TiO2 pigment production**: 30,000 t/yr capacity
- **Iron sulfate byproduct (total)**: ~100,000 t/yr
  - ~24,000 t/yr to Kemira Chemicals
  - Remaining: other KRONOS ecochem customers + landfill stabilization
- **Thin acid residue**: Used for neutralizing hazardous inorganic waste (fly ash from FREVAR and Kvitebjorn Bio-El)
- Previous environmental issue: pre-1980, iron sulfate was discharged into the sea
- 2016 incident: ~100 tonnes illegal thin acid discharge

### Kemira Chemicals AS
- **Input**: ~24,000 t/yr iron sulfate from Kronos Titan
- **Output**: 90,000 t/yr total coagulant products
- **Primary product**: Iron chloride sulfate (jernklorsulfat)
- Capacity expansion 2024-2025 (new reactor + aluminium hydroxide storage)

### Denofa AS
- **Soybean intake**: ~420,000-450,000 t/yr
- **Products**: Soybean meal, crude soybean oil, lecithin (Denothin 62)
- **Byproducts**: Soybean hulls
- **Waste streams**: Wastewater, cooling water (significant volumes)
- Water exchange potential studied by NORSUS (2015)

### Stene Stal Gjenvinning AS
- **Annual throughput**: ~50,000 t/yr recycled material
- **Materials processed**: Iron scrap, ferrous metals, non-ferrous metals, scrap vehicles, steel constructions
- **Output**: Processed iron scrap, sorted metal fractions to foundries, smelters, steel works
- ISO 14001 certified (first Norwegian recycler, since 1998)

### Metallco Kabel AS
- **Annual throughput**: ~14,000 t/yr used cables (target: 18,000+ t/yr)
- **Cable plastic byproduct**: 6,000-8,000 t/yr (exploring new uses)
- **Output**: Pure copper, aluminum fractions
- **LIBS sorting facility**: 15 t/hr capacity, 100 MNOK investment
- Delivers purer metal fractions for domestic processing (vs. export)

### Metallco Stene AS
- Co-located metal shredding facility
- Part of Stene Stal group
- Combined revenue with Metallco Kabel and Metallco Sortering: ~1 billion NOK

### NG Metall (Norsk Gjenvinning Metall / NG Nordic)
- **Shredder capacity**: 140 t/hr (Norway's most powerful)
- **Daily throughput**: Equivalent to ~1,000 scrapped cars/day
- **NG Nordic total (9 facilities)**: 350,000 t/yr waste processed, 253,875 t/yr new raw materials produced
- **Fredrikstad facility**: Largest in Norway, estimated ~250,000 t/yr across Norwegian operations
- **Materials**: Steel, metals, electronic waste, batteries, cables, scrapped vehicles
- **Output**: Recycled metal sold to smelters and steel works worldwide

### Batteriretur AS
- Battery recycling (lead-acid and lithium)
- Located at Ora
- Volumes: Not specified in sources

### Total Ora Area Waste/Material Flows (2021)
- **Total waste received and handled**: ~520,000 t/yr
- **Recycled material sent outside Ora**: ~390,000 t/yr
- **Ora handles 43-81% of Norway's relevant waste streams**

### NORSUS Water Study (2015)
- Studied water exchange potential between FREVAR, Denofa, and Kronos Titan
- Found water systems operate more linearly than energy/waste networks
- Significant cooling water demand across facilities
- Data collected on water type, usage, volume, temperature, quality
- Conclusion: Room for improvement in water symbiosis
- Detailed exchange potential documented in accompanying Excel file (not available)

### Circular Water Flow
- FREVAR receives heated cooling water from industry, reuses it in water treatment processes

### Denofa Water
- Significant cooling water volumes used in soybean processing
- Wastewater streams studied for exchange potential

### Kronos Titan Water
- Historical wastewater discharge caused water quality issues (reduced pH, metal overconcentrations)
- Water consumption volumes not publicly available
