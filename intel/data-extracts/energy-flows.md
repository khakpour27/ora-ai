## Waste-to-Energy Production

### FREVAR Energigjenvinningsanlegg (Energy Recovery Plant)
- **Commissioned**: 1984
- **Annual waste processed**: ~73,000 tonnes/year (FREVAR website); operating permit: ~92,000 tonnes/year
- **Processing rate**: ~5.5 tonnes/hour per line (2 lines)
- **Total energy produced**: 210 GWh/year
- **Energy delivered**: ~190 GWh/year
- **Energy efficiency**: 99.3%
- **Annual steam delivered**: ~230,000 tonnes/year
- **Oil equivalent replaced**: 22,000 tonnes/year
- **Steam temperature**: ~320°C (delivered), ~385°C (produced)
- **Steam pressure**: ~18 bar (delivered), ~40 bar (produced)
- **Each line**: ~17.5 tonnes steam/hour
- **Availability**: ~93.35%
- Source: `frevar-energy.md`

### SAREN Energy BIO-EL (Kvitebjørn Bio-El)
- **Operational since**: 2008
- **Original capacity (2008-2024)**: 22 MW installed, 60,000 tonnes waste/year, 170 GWh/year gross
- **Expanded capacity (Dec 2024+)**: 37 MW installed, 105,000 tonnes waste/year, ~300 GWh/year gross (estimated)
- **Third waste line**: Commissioned December 2024, nearly doubling capacity
- **Energy output streams**: District heating (hot water), industrial steam, electricity (grid)
- **Waste sourcing**: >2/3 from UK (via Borg Havn)
- **Operated by**: FREVAR KF staff
- Source: `saren-energi.md`

### Combined Waste Incineration (Øra total, 2021)
- **Total residual waste incinerated**: ~130,000 tonnes (FREVAR + SAREN/Kvitebjørn combined)
- **Total waste-based steam**: ~250 GWh (~32% of Øra total energy)
- Source: `norsus-ora-symbiosis-data-2021.md`

## Biogas Production

### FREVAR Biogassanlegg
- **Raw biogas production**: ~4 million m³/year
- **Upgraded biogas (biomethane)**: ~2.5 million m³/year (fuel quality)
- **Feedstock capacity**: 30,000 tonnes/year
  - Packaged substrate: 6,000 tonnes/year
  - Liquid feedstock: 24,000 tonnes/year
  - Sludge intake: up to 2,000 tonnes/year
- **Upgrading capacity**: 400 Nm³/h compressed fuel-quality biogas
- **Digester volume**: 7,400 m³ total (3 reactors: 2,200 + 2,200 + 3,000 m³)
- **Process temperature**: up to 70°C (thermophilic)
- **Residence time**: up to 21 days
- **Distribution**: Via Gasum (Finland), reaches Norway and Sweden
- **First profitable year**: 2025 (1.3 million NOK profit)
- **Constructed**: 2014; production doubled from initial capacity
- **Steam input from FREVAR Energigjenvinning**: ~5 GWh
- Source: `frevar-biogas.md`, `ncce-frevar-energy-motor-ora.md`

### Biogas Expansion Potential (Nedre Glomma region)
- **Current climate benefit**: up to 8,400 tonnes CO2-eq/year
- **2030 scenario**: up to 11,400 tonnes CO2-eq/year
- **Regional biogas potential**: up to 100 GWh capacity
- **Target**: 30% of livestock manure through biogas plants by 2030
- Source: `norsus-biogas-potential-nedre-glomma.md`

## Company Energy Profiles

### Kronos Titan AS
- **Annual energy consumption**: ~310 GWh (some sources cite ~365 GWh, "~1 GWh/day")
- **~40% of Øra's total 782 GWh** — the single largest consumer
- **Energy breakdown**:
  | Source | Amount | Notes |
  |--------|--------|-------|
  | Heavy fuel oil / LNG | ~150 GWh | Transitioning from oil to LNG |
  | Steam from FREVAR/Kvitebjørn | ~130 GWh | Waste-to-energy steam |
  | Electricity | ~30 GWh | Grid electricity |
- **Renewable percentage**: 20-30% of total energy from renewable sources
- **LNG transition**: Replaced 7,000 of 14,500 tonnes heavy fuel oil with LNG
  - Savings: 5,500 tonnes CO2/year and 50 tonnes NOx/year
- **Full electrification**: Planned but blocked by insufficient grid capacity (not before 2030)
- **Process temperatures**: Rotary kilns 450°C inlet → 970°C outlet
- **ISO 50001:2018 certified** (Energy Management)
- Source: `kronos-titan.md`, `kronos-kemira-symbiosis.md`

### Denofa AS
- **Annual steam consumption**: ~100 GWh
- **External steam procurement**: ~80 GWh from FREVAR and Kvitebjørn Bio-El
- **Own steam production**: 45-50 GWh from own boiler house
- **Own boiler types**:
  - Electrode boiler (primary, electric)
  - Gas boiler (backup, LNG)
- **Grid constraint**: Insufficient regional power grid prevents full electrode boiler use
- **Supplies steam to**: Neighboring companies on Øra (including Adesso)
- Source: `denofa.md`, `frevar-denofa-steam.md`

### Fredrikstad Fjernvarme AS (District Heating)
- **Heat delivered**: >88 GWh/year
- **Cooling delivered**: 2 GWh/year
- **Energy source**: 95% recovered waste heat from waste incineration at Øra
- **Primary heat producers**: FREVAR and SAREN Energi Bio-El
- **Proposed expansion**: 160 GWh production, 100 MW power (NVE concession data)
- **Network coverage**: Fredrikstad city center, Værste, Seut, Sellebakk, Rolvsøy, Øra, FMV area, Holmen-Seut, Trara-Valle
- **Mandatory connection**: New buildings >1,000 m² within concession area
- Source: `fredrikstad-fjernvarme.md`

### FREVAR Biogass
- **Steam received from FREVAR Energigjenvinning**: ~5 GWh

## Seasonal Patterns

No specific seasonal data was found in the web pages. However, the following can be inferred:

- **District heating demand** is strongly seasonal — higher in winter (October-April), lower in summer
- **Industrial steam demand** (Kronos Titan, Denofa) is likely more constant year-round (process heat)
- **FREVAR operates continuously** (6-shift operation, ~93.35% availability) — waste incineration is not seasonal
- **Fredrikstad Fjernvarme** operates year-round but with variable load
- **District cooling** (2 GWh in Værste area) peaks in summer

## CCS Energy Implications

### Borg CO2 Cluster
- **CO2 capture potential**: ~630,000 tonnes CO2/year across three Øra locations
- **Significant biogenic CO2** (bio-CCS) from waste incineration
- **Partners**: 18 companies including FREVAR, SAREN/Kvitebjørn Bio-El, Borg Havn
- **Infrastructure**: CO2 intermediate storage at Fredrikstad harbor → ships to Øygarden → North Sea storage (2,600m below seabed)
- **Cost**: 1,250 NOK per tonne captured CO2
- **Energy penalty**: CCS will consume additional energy from the incineration plants (exact figures not available — data gap)
- Source: `frevar-ccs-carbon-capture.md`, `ncce-borg-co2-ccs-ora.md`, `ncce-ccs-cluster-ora-regional.md`, `norsus-ccs-cluster-ora.md`

### SAREN Energy CCS
- Dedicated CCS staff: Project Developer and Business Developer
- Active pursuit of carbon capture at waste-to-energy plants
- Source: `saren-energi.md`

## Energy Optimization Opportunities

### Identified in Research
1. **Kronos Titan full electrification** — replace ~150 GWh of LNG/oil with electricity; blocked by grid capacity (not before 2030)
2. **Denofa electrode boiler expansion** — replace LNG backup with full electric; also blocked by grid capacity
3. **SAREN capacity expansion** — third line (Dec 2024) nearly doubles waste-to-energy capacity from 170 to ~300 GWh/year, enabling more fossil fuel displacement
4. **Fredrikstad Fjernvarme expansion** — proposed 160 GWh production, 100 MW power (NVE concession)
5. **Regional biogas expansion** — potential 100 GWh capacity in Nedre Glomma region
6. **CCS integration** — capture 630,000 tonnes CO2/year, but adds energy penalty
7. **Water stream exchange** — cooling water loops between FREVAR, Denofa, Kronos Titan (NORSUS 2015 study identified potential)
8. **Ironworks slag as sand replacement** — SAREN tests reducing virgin sand from 1,600 to 600 tonnes/year

### Grid Capacity Bottleneck
Multiple companies (Kronos Titan, Denofa) cite insufficient regional electricity grid capacity as the primary barrier to electrification and decarbonization. This is the single largest obstacle to further energy optimization at Øra.
